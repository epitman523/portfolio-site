Severe Weather Quiz App

About Project

This app is a project through Bloc and was meant to demonstrate our ability of combining all of the lessons we had into a functioning web app. We were allowed to pick the topic and being from the Midwest I thought that severe weather would be a relavent topic. Most people know what to do at home, though. I decided that it would be more helpful and informative to do sever weather tips while traveling.

Since this was an assignment, there were several requirements:

  • It must have a landing page with a button to start the quiz
  • There must be at least 5 questions that are sequential and show one at a time
  • Users must be able to see what question number they were on as well as their score
  • When the user submits their answer, they should recieve feedback on whether they're correct or incorrect
  • There must be a overall score at the end of the quiz, as well as a way to retake the quiz

Starting with sketches, I fleshed out the basic concept of the web app. Next I built a graybox wireframe to establish the layout. From there I started building the HTML and CSS, paying close attention to a11y's best web practices. Once the app was mostly built out, I began the JavaScript and jQuery, which was by far the most labor intensive part of the assignment. All of the questions and answers are dynamically loaded into the page. The question number and score are in the bottom of the main section of the quiz and I also included a static pagination to show progress.
